# Pitch Script (3 minutes)

## 1. Opening story (20s)
Pilots don’t learn to fly only from lectures. They train in flight simulators — thousands of safe,
repeatable, measured encounters before they ever carry passengers. Medical students deserve the
same. Today, most of them don’t get it.

## 2. The problem (25s)
Medical school cohorts are growing, but instructors, patient access, teaching time, and simulation
facilities are fixed. The result is unequal practice, less hands-on training, and thin, inconsistent
feedback. The scarcest resource in medical education isn’t content — it’s **expert teaching time**.

## 3. Why existing tools are incomplete (25s)
Simulation platforms like Body Interact teach great cases. Knowledge platforms like AMBOSS teach
facts. Logbooks track activity. But **none of them connect** realistic encounters, real voice
practice, sensitive-communication feedback, instructor support, and curriculum-level analytics into
one loop. There’s no operating system for clinical teaching.

## 4. The solution (20s)
**ClinFlight OS** is a clinical-reasoning flight simulator and a teaching command center. Students
practice realistic voice-first encounters. Instructors get targeted debrief support. Program
directors see learning gaps across the whole cohort. One adaptive loop.

## 5. The demo (60s)
Meet Maya, a 4th-year. The system detected she struggles with sensitive-question delivery, so it
recommends the right case: David, 72, dizzy and falling. Watch what happens when she asks the
judgmental way — *“Are you taking your meds properly?”* David shuts down. The hidden concern stays
locked. Trust drops. Now the better way — *“Many people stop medications because of side effects, and
that’s completely understandable…”* David admits he stopped his blood-pressure pill a week ago. The
**hidden concern unlocks**. She orders orthostatic vitals, submits her reasoning, and gets an
OSCE-style score with **evidence** and a **replay** of the decisive moment.

## 6. Impact (20s)
For the **student**: deliberate practice with instant, fair, formative feedback. For the
**instructor**: an AI debrief guide that says exactly where to spend ten minutes. For the **sim
center**: *“62% of the cohort missed medication adherence — they know what to ask, but phrase it
judgmentally.”* That’s a curriculum fix, not a guess.

## 7. Why it scales (15s)
It’s web-based, runs on deterministic local logic with no dependency on flaky APIs, and turns every
encounter — simulated *or* real rotation — into measurable, de-identified learning. More students,
same instructors, better teaching.

## 8. Future roadmap (15s)
Real speech-to-text and prosody, voice-to-voice and avatar patients, Hebrew/Arabic/English cases,
LMS and OSCE-station integration, and an instructor-authored case library — deployable in a real
simulation center.

## Closing line
**ClinFlight OS does not replace instructors. It helps them know exactly where their time matters
most.** Because clinical competence is not only knowing the right answer — it’s asking the right
question, in the right way, at the right moment.
