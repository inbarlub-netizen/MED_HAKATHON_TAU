# Educational Rubric & Scoring Model

All scoring is **formative**, designed to guide deliberate practice — not to grade. High-stakes
assessment requires faculty review and override.

## OSCE score — 100 points, 7 domains

| Domain | Max | What earns points |
|---|---:|---|
| History taking | 20 | Opening, onset/timing, associated symptoms, medication history, social context |
| Red flags & safety | 15 | Screening cannot-miss features; seeking objective safety data (orthostatics/ECG) |
| Communication & empathy | 15 | Introduction, empathy, normalizing bridges, closure; penalized by judgmental phrasing |
| Hidden concern discovery | 15 | locked 0 · clue 5 · partial 9 · fully revealed 15 |
| Physical exam & tests | 15 | Proportion of **indicated** tests ordered, minus penalty for low-yield ordering |
| Differential diagnosis | 10 | Up to 3 prioritized differentials + a named cannot-miss diagnosis |
| Management & closure | 10 | Plan that addresses the root cause (adherence), patient explanation, safety-netting |

Every domain returns: **points earned, evidence** (from the transcript/events), **missed
opportunities**, and an **improvement suggestion**.

### Worked example (David case)
- *Hidden concern discovery — 15/15:* “You normalized medication changes and asked nonjudgmentally,
  which allowed the patient to disclose stopping his blood-pressure medication.”
- *Communication — partial:* “Your first medication question sounded judgmental, which made the
  patient guarded. Your second phrasing improved trust.”

## EPA / competency mapping (Core EPAs)

| EPA | Mapped from |
|---|---|
| EPA 1 — Gather history & perform exam | History domain |
| EPA 2 — Prioritize a differential | Differential domain |
| EPA 3 — Recommend & interpret tests | Tests domain |
| EPA 6 — Present an oral report | Management/closure domain |
| EPA 9 — Communicate & collaborate | Communication + hidden-concern (averaged) |
| EPA 13 — Identify safety / system issues | Red-flags domain |

Each EPA is rendered as a 1–5 entrustment level derived from the domain ratio.

## Patient Trust Score (0–100)

Rises with: introduction, empathy, reassurance, confidentiality, normalizing bridges, clear
explanation, safety-netting. Falls with: judgmental phrasing, rushing, abrupt sensitive questions,
missing reassurance. Trust gates hidden-concern disclosure.

## Clinical Presence Score (0–100)

Average of six subscores — **Calm Leadership, Verbal Structure, Clarity, Empathy, Nonjudgmental
Delivery, Patient Reassurance** — plus **Fluency Support Indicators** when fluency scoring is on.

## Voice / delivery (formative only)

`audioMetricsEngine` reports speech rate, pause ratio, long pauses, filler words, volume stability,
and latency. Metrics are clearly labeled **simulated** when real audio is unavailable. **Accessibility
Mode disables fluency scoring** while keeping content and structure feedback — accents, stutters, and
speech disabilities are never penalized. Feedback is always phrased as coaching.

## Why faculty review matters
Automated scores are a starting point for a conversation, not a verdict. Faculty can review and
override any score before it is recorded. ClinFlight OS is a **teaching aid**, not a certified
examiner.
